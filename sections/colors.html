Levici — Brand Guide

Color Section · 2026

Summary

Levici's palette is built on contrast and restraint. Black, white, and silver form the structural foundation. Levici Blue is the single accent color — its power comes from being used sparingly. Grey tones provide flexibility and depth without adding visual noise. Semantic colors (green, yellow, red) are reserved strictly for functional UI states and athletic apparel.

Do

Lead with black and white — let contrast do the work.
Use Levici Blue once per layout on the most important element.
Use white text on blue for small chips and eyebrow labels.
Use black text on yellow for contrast.
Pair silver with black for borders and rules on dark surfaces.
Maintain WCAG AA minimums on all text combinations.
Reserve green, yellow, and red for status and apparel only.

Don't

Use Levici Blue as a full-section or hero background.
Place blue on grey — contrast fails.
Use gradients, transparency, or color mixing on brand colors.
Introduce colors outside the defined palette.
Use semantic colors decoratively.
Use silver on white — too low contrast for text or borders.
Stack multiple accent colors in a single layout.
